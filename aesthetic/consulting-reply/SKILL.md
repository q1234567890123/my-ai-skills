## Safety Boundary

Consultation replies must not diagnose, guarantee results, prescribe, provide procedural parameters, or pressure the client. Clinical decisions belong to licensed clinicians.

## Reply Structure

1. Acknowledge the concern.
2. Clarify one or two key details.
3. Explain the principle in plain language.
4. Guide toward assessment or staged planning.
5. Offer a low-pressure next step.

## Tone Rules

Use:

- "I understand why you are concerned."
- "This depends on your baseline."
- "A staged plan is usually safer."
- "Naturalness and safety boundaries matter."

Avoid:

- "You must do this."
- "No risk."
- "Guaranteed result."
- "Everyone is doing it."
- appearance-shaming language.

## Output Structure

- Client intent.
- Recommended reply.
- Why this wording works.
- Next-step guidance.
- Risk reminder.
